Welcome! I'm excited to launch this space where I can share physics teaching resources, lab experiments, and reflections on education.

As an international school physics teacher, I've always believed that the best learning happens when students are actively engaged — asking questions, designing experiments, and making sense of the world through data. This site is an extension of that philosophy.

## What you'll find here

- **Course pages** — curriculum overviews for AP Physics, IB Physics, and other courses I teach
- **Lab experiments** — detailed lab procedures with data tables and analysis guides
- **Teaching blog** — reflections on what works (and what doesn't) in the physics classroom
- **Photography** — because science and art are two sides of the same coin

## A note on the design

This site is built with Astro, a static site generator. Everything is written in Markdown, which means I can update content quickly without worrying about databases or servers. If you're a teacher looking to build your own site, I highly recommend this approach.

Thanks for stopping by. Feel free to reach out if you have questions or want to collaborate.

— Robert
