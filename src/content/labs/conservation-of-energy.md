## Objective

To verify the conservation of mechanical energy by measuring the kinetic and potential energy of a pendulum at different points in its swing.

## Theory

For a system with only conservative forces, the total mechanical energy is conserved:

$$E_{\text{total}} = KE + PE = \text{constant}$$

For a pendulum:
- Gravitational potential energy: $PE = mgh$
- Kinetic energy: $KE = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$

At the highest point, energy is entirely potential. At the lowest point, energy is entirely kinetic (if we set $h=0$ at the bottom).

## Procedure

1. Measure the mass of the pendulum bob.
2. Measure the length of the pendulum string.
3. Release the pendulum from a measured angle $\theta$.
4. Calculate the initial height: $h = L(1 - \cos\theta)$
5. Use the photogate to measure the speed at the lowest point.
6. Calculate $KE$ and $PE$ at both positions.
7. Repeat for different release angles.

## Analysis

Compare the initial potential energy with the kinetic energy at the lowest point. The percentage difference indicates how well energy is conserved in your setup. Discuss sources of energy loss (air resistance, friction at the pivot).

## Extension

Repeat the experiment with a heavier bob. Does mass affect the conservation of energy? Why or why not?
