## Course Overview

AP Physics 1 is an algebra-based introductory college-level physics course. Students cultivate their understanding of physics through inquiry-based investigations as they explore these topics.

## Topics Covered

### 1. Kinematics
- Position, velocity, and acceleration
- Motion graphs
- Projectile motion
- Relative motion

### 2. Dynamics
- Newton's Laws of Motion
- Forces and free-body diagrams
- Friction and drag
- Applications of $F = ma$

### 3. Circular Motion & Gravitation
- Uniform circular motion
- Centripetal acceleration: $a_c = \frac{v^2}{r}$
- Newton's Law of Gravitation: $F_g = G\frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}$

### 4. Energy
- Work-energy theorem
- Conservation of mechanical energy
- Kinetic and potential energy
- Power: $P = \frac{W}{t}$

### 5. Momentum
- Impulse-momentum theorem: $J = \Delta p = F \Delta t$
- Conservation of momentum
- Elastic and inelastic collisions

### 6. Simple Harmonic Motion
- Hooke's Law: $F_s = -kx$
- Period of a mass-spring system: $T = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{m}{k}}$
- Pendulum motion: $T = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}$

## Lab Requirements

Students complete at least 12 inquiry-based lab experiments throughout the year, maintaining a lab notebook with procedures, data, and analysis.
